# IV. The Project

**As the race to AGI intensifies, the national security state will get involved. The USG will wake from its slumber, and by 27/28 we'll get some form of government AGI project. No startup can handle superintelligence. Somewhere in a SCIF, the endgame will be on.**

The chapter opens with a Spencer R. Weart quote marveling at how hundreds of power plants, thousands of bombs, and tens of thousands of people massed in national establishments can be traced back to a few people sitting at laboratory benches discussing the peculiar behavior of one type of atom.

## The Category Error of Current AI Governance

Many plans for "AI governance" are being put forth -- licensing frontier AI systems, safety standards, public cloud with a few hundred million in compute for academics. These seem well-intentioned but represent a **category error**.

It is an **insane** proposition that the US government will let a random SF startup develop superintelligence. Imagine if we had developed atomic bombs by letting Uber just improvise.

Superintelligence -- AI systems much smarter than humans -- will have vast power, from developing novel weaponry to driving an explosion in economic growth. It will be the locus of international competition; a lead of months potentially decisive in military conflict.

The great minds of San Francisco hope they can control the destiny of the demon they are birthing. Right now, they still can -- they are among the few with situational awareness. But in the next few years, the world will wake up. So too will the national security state. History will make a triumphant return.

## The Path to The Project

### The COVID Analogy

Late February to mid-March 2020: it was clear we were on the COVID exponential, a plague about to sweep the country. Almost nobody took it seriously. The Mayor of New York was dismissing COVID fears as racism. Yet within just a few weeks, the entire country shut down and Congress had appropriated trillions of dollars (literally >10% of GDP).

The next few years in AI will feel similar. We are in the midgame now. 2023 was already a wild shift -- AGI went from a fringe topic to the subject of major Senate hearings and world leader summits.

### The Escalation Timeline

- **2025/2026**: The next truly shocking step-changes. AI will drive $100B+ annual revenues for big tech companies and outcompete PhDs in raw problem-solving smarts. $10T valuations and AI mania everywhere
- **2027/2028**: Models trained on the $100B+ cluster. Full-fledged AI agents / drop-in remote workers will start to widely automate software engineering and other cognitive jobs. Each year, acceleration will feel dizzying
- A consensus will gradually form among leading scientists, executives, and government officials: we are on the cusp of AGI, on the cusp of an intelligence explosion, on the cusp of superintelligence
- The first genuinely terrifying demonstrations of AI will arrive -- perhaps helping novices make bioweapons, or autonomously hacking critical systems
- It will become clear this is an utterly decisive military technology
- The CCP will take note and likely launch a formidable AGI effort
- Perhaps the discovery of the CCP's infiltration of America's leading AI labs causes a big stir

### The Manhattan Project Parallel

Somewhere around 26/27, the mood in Washington will become somber. From the halls of the Pentagon to backroom Congressional briefings will ring the obvious question: do we need an AGI Manhattan Project? Slowly at first, then all at once, it will become clear: this is happening, things are going to get wild, this is the most important challenge for the national security of the United States since the invention of the atomic bomb.

As with COVID, as with the Manhattan Project, the government will be incredibly late and hamfisted. After Einstein's letter to the President in 1939, an Advisory Committee on Uranium was formed, but officials were incompetent: Fermi only got $6k (about $135k in today's dollars) to support his research, received only after months of waiting. Szilard believed the project was delayed for at least a year by bureaucratic sluggishness. A full-scale atomic bomb effort did not launch until December 1941.

**But by late 26/27/28, it will be underway.** The core AGI research team (a few hundred researchers) will move to a secure location; the trillion-dollar cluster will be built in record-speed; The Project will be on.

## Why The Project Is the Only Way

The author has no illusions about government limitations and poor incentives. He is a big believer in the American private sector and would almost never advocate for heavy government involvement in technology or industry.

But AI labs are still startups. We simply should not expect startups to be equipped to handle superintelligence. When a technology becomes this important for national security, we will need the USG.

### Superintelligence as the US's Most Important National Defense Project

Within years, superintelligence would completely shake up the military balance of power. By the early 2030s, the entirety of the US arsenal will probably be obsolete -- not just modernization, but a wholesale replacement.

The development of AGI will fall in a category more like nukes than the internet. Yes, it is dual-use -- but nuclear technology was dual-use too. In the fog of the AGI endgame, national security will be the primary backdrop.

### What The Project Requires

- Government involvement to defend against all-out espionage threats
- A sane chain of command to ensure even a semblance of responsible oversight (you cannot have random CEOs or random nonprofit boards with the nuclear button)
- Government to manage the severe safety challenges of superintelligence
- Government to deploy superintelligence to defend against whatever extreme threats unfold
- Government to mobilize a democratic coalition to win the race

### A Sane Chain of Command

The power and challenges of superintelligence will fall into a very different reference class than anything we are used to from tech companies. This should not be under the unilateral command of a random CEO:

- In the private-labs-developing-superintelligence world, individual CEOs would have the power to literally **coup the US government** (AI lab employees will have been mostly automated by this point)
- Imagine if Elon Musk had final command of the nuclear arsenal, or if a random nonprofit board could seize control
- As a society, we have decided democratic governments should control the military; superintelligence will be the most powerful military weapon
- The radical proposal is not The Project -- the radical proposal is taking a bet on private AI CEOs wielding military power

The Burkean argument applies: the institutions, constitutions, laws, courts, checks and balances, norms, and common dedication to the liberal democratic order that check the power of the government have withstood the test of hundreds of years. Special AI lab governance structures collapsed the first time they were tested.

### The Civilian Uses of Superintelligence

This does not mean civilian applications will be permanently reserved for the government. Historical precedents:
- Nuclear energy flourished as private projects (1960s-70s) after being first harnessed as a government project
- Boeing made the B-29 (the most expensive WWII defense R&D project) before using that technology for the commercial Boeing 707
- Radar, satellites, rockets, gene technology, WWII factories all followed this pattern

Even in worlds with The Project, a private, pluralistic, market-based, flourishing ecosystem of civilian applications of superintelligence will have its day.

### Security

On the current course, we may as well give up on having any American AGI effort -- China can promptly steal everything. It is not even clear we will get to "North Korea-proof" security on the current course. In the private-startups world, superintelligence would proliferate to dozens of rogue states.

If we are to eventually face the full force of Chinese espionage (stealing the weights being the MSS's #1 priority), it is probably impossible for a private company to get good enough security. It will require extensive cooperation from the US intelligence community: extreme vetting, constant monitoring, working from a SCIF, reduced freedom to leave, and infrastructure only the government can provide.

### Safety

There are many ways to mess this up -- from the superalignment problem to controlling risks of new means of mass destruction. Some AI labs claim commitment to safety, but they are startups with the usual commercial incentives. Competition could push all of them to race through the intelligence explosion, and there will be actors willing to throw safety by the wayside.

Regulation will not be sufficient either. What is necessary will be less like spending years doing careful evaluations and more like **fighting a war** -- an insane year where the situation shifts extremely rapidly every week, in which hard calls based on ambiguous data will be life-or-death. "Some of our alignment measurements are looking ambiguous, we don't really understand what's going on anymore... should we delay the next training run by 3 months to get more confidence on safety -- but oh no, the latest intelligence reports indicate China stole our weights and is racing ahead on their own intelligence explosion, what should we do?"

### Stabilizing the International Situation

The intelligence explosion and its immediate aftermath will bring forth one of the most volatile and tense situations mankind has ever faced. The task at hand will not be to build cool products but to somehow, desperately, make it through this period.

We will need the government project to:
- Win the race against authoritarian powers
- Give the clear lead and breathing room to navigate the perils
- Prevent instant theft of superintelligence model weights
- Bundle Western efforts (best scientists, every GPU, trillions in cluster buildouts in the US)
- Protect datacenters against adversary sabotage or outright attack
- Develop and enforce a nonproliferation regime
- Subvert Russia, North Korea, Iran, and terrorist groups from using superintelligence for weaponry
- Harden security of critical infrastructure, military, and government
- Stabilize the offense/defense balance of advances in biology
- Develop tools to safely control superintelligence
- Shut down rogue superintelligences from others' uncareful projects

## The Project Is Inevitable; Whether It's Good Is Not

The main claim is descriptive, not normative: whether we like it or not, superintelligence will not look like an SF startup and will be primarily in the domain of national security. Most people in SF, once they consider this possibility, agree it seems obvious.

### Key Free Variables

**When, not if**: Does the government realize what is happening before or during an intelligence explosion? Earlier is better. We dearly need those couple years to do the security crash program, get key officials up to speed, and build a functioning merged lab. It will be far more chaotic if the government only steps in at the very end.

**The international coalition**: Both a tighter alliance of democracies and a broader benefit-sharing offer to the rest of the world.
- The former might look like the **Quebec Agreement** (a secret pact between Churchill and Roosevelt to pool resources to develop nuclear weapons): bringing in the UK (DeepMind), Japan and South Korea (chip supply chain), and NATO/other core democratic allies
- The latter might look like **Atoms for Peace**, the IAEA, and the NPT: offering peaceful benefits of superintelligence to a broader group of countries in exchange for refraining from their own superintelligence projects and accepting restrictions on dual-use applications

**Competence**: Perhaps the most important free variable is whether the inevitable government project will be competent. How will the checks and balances work? What does a sane chain of command look like? Scarcely any attention has gone into figuring this out. Almost all other AI lab and AI governance politicking is a sideshow. **This is the ballgame.**

## The Endgame

By 27/28, the endgame will be on. By 28/29 the intelligence explosion will be underway; by 2030, we will have summoned superintelligence in all its power and might.

Whoever they put in charge of The Project is going to have a hell of a task:
- Build AGI, and build it fast
- Put the American economy on wartime footing to make hundreds of millions of GPUs
- Lock it all down, weed out the spies, fend off all-out attacks by the CCP
- Manage a hundred million AGIs furiously automating AI research, making a decade's leaps in a year, soon producing AI systems vastly smarter than the smartest humans
- Keep things together enough that this does not go off the rails and produce rogue superintelligence
- Use those superintelligences to develop whatever new technologies are needed to stabilize the situation and stay ahead of adversaries
- Rapidly remake US forces to integrate those
- Navigate what will likely be the tensest international situation ever seen

For those who get the call to come along for the ride, it will be stressful. But it will be our duty to serve the free world -- and all of humanity. SF already feels like a peculiar AI-researcher-college-town; probably this will not be so different, except the stakes will be all too real.

**See you in the desert, friends.**
