# II. From AGI to Superintelligence: the Intelligence Explosion

**AI progress won't stop at human-level.** Hundreds of millions of AGIs could automate AI research, compressing a decade of algorithmic progress (5+ OOMs) into 1 year. We would rapidly go from human-level to vastly superhuman AI systems. The power — and the peril — of superintelligence would be dramatic.

> "Let an ultraintelligent machine be defined as a machine that can far surpass all the intellectual activities of any man however clever. Since the design of machines is one of these intellectual activities, an ultraintelligent machine could design even better machines; there would then unquestionably be an 'intelligence explosion,' and the intelligence of man would be left far behind. Thus the first ultraintelligent machine is the last invention that man need ever make."
> — I. J. Good (1965)

## The Bomb and The Super

Historical parallel: the atomic bomb was dramatic, but the hydrogen bomb (The Super) multiplied yields a thousand-fold — a single bomb with more explosive power than all of WWII combined. So it will be with AGI and Superintelligence.

## Automating AI Research

**We don't need to automate everything — just AI research.** The job of an AI researcher is straightforward: read ML literature, come up with ideas, implement experiments, interpret results, repeat. This falls squarely within what simple extrapolations of current AI capabilities could handle by 2027.

Key breakthroughs have been surprisingly simple: "add some normalization" (LayerNorm/BatchNorm), "do f(x)+x instead of f(x)" (residual connections), "fix an implementation bug" (Kaplan → Chinchilla scaling laws).

### The Scale of Automated Research

By 2027, GPU fleets should enable running **100 million human-researcher-equivalents**, running day and night. Automated AI researchers will have enormous advantages:

- Read every ML paper ever written, accumulate millennia of experience
- Write millions of lines of complex code, keep entire codebases in context
- Spend human-decades checking every line for bugs and optimizations
- Clone and replicate instantly (no onboarding bottleneck)
- Share context across vast numbers of copies
- Work at 10x-100x human speed (via inference speedups)

**100 million automated researchers each working at 100x human speed** — they'll each do a year's worth of work in a few days.

### Compressing a Decade into a Year

This could easily **compress a decade of algorithmic advances into a year** — yielding 5+ OOMs of algorithmic progress. That's another GPT-2-to-GPT-4-sized capability jump, *on top of* AGI-level systems. A qualitative jump from ~preschooler to ~smart high schooler, *on top of* Alec Radford.

## Possible Bottlenecks

### 1. Limited Compute
The most important bottleneck. Millions of researchers won't have proportionally more experiment compute. But the automated researchers can mitigate this by:
- Testing at small scale and extrapolating via scaling laws
- Finding enormous "unhobbling" gains that don't require big pretraining runs
- Finding efficiency gains that let them run more experiments
- Being far more efficient — spending centuries-equivalent of thinking before running experiments
- Having superhuman ML intuitions (trained on millions of experiment results)

### 2. Complementarities / Long Tail
Automating 70% of something means the remaining 30% becomes the bottleneck. The last 10% of an AI researcher's job might be hard to automate. But this likely only delays things by a couple years:
- 2026/27: Proto-automated-engineer, speeds up work 1.5x-2x
- 2027/28: Proto-automated-researchers, automate >90%, speed up progress 3x+
- 2028/29: 10x+ pace of progress → superintelligence

### 3. Inherent Limits to Algorithmic Progress
5 more OOMs of algorithmic efficiency seems well within the realm of possibilities — it would just require another decade of trend algorithmic efficiencies. Current architectures are still very rudimentary. Biological references suggest huge headroom.

### 4. Ideas Get Harder to Find
While ideas do get harder to find, the ~million-fold increase in research effort far exceeds what would be needed to merely sustain progress. The empirical evidence favors explosive/accelerating progress, not just maintenance of current rates.

## The Power of Superintelligence

The AI systems by the end of this decade would be unimaginably powerful:

- **Quantitatively superhuman**: Run a civilization of billions, think orders of magnitude faster, master any domain, write trillions of lines of code
- **Qualitatively superhuman**: Produce completely novel, creative behaviors beyond human understanding — like AlphaGo's famous move 37

### Broader Implications

- **AI capabilities explosion**: Solve remaining automation bottlenecks in all cognitive domains
- **Solve robotics**: Primarily an ML algorithms problem that superintelligent AI researchers will crack
- **Dramatically accelerate scientific and technological progress**: Compress the next century of R&D into years
- **An industrial and economic explosion**: Economic growth rates of 30%/year and beyond — a fundamental shift in growth regime

| Growth mode | Date began | Doubling time (years) |

| Hunting | 2,000,000 B.C. | 230,000 |
| Farming | 4700 B.C. | 860 |
| Science/commerce | 1730 A.D. | 58 |
| Industry | 1903 A.D. | 15 |
| Superintelligence? | 2030 A.D.? | ??? |

- **Decisive military advantage**: Completely new weapons, invulnerable missile defense, novel WMDs. Like 21st century militaries fighting a 19th century brigade.
- **Power to overthrow governments**: Whoever controls superintelligence could potentially seize control from pre-superintelligence forces.

## Conclusion

The intelligence explosion and the immediate post-superintelligence period will be one of the most volatile, tense, dangerous, and wildest periods ever in human history. And by the end of the decade, we'll likely be in the midst of it.

We must once again confront the possibility of a chain reaction. Perhaps it sounds speculative. But among senior scientists at AI labs, many see a rapid intelligence explosion as strikingly plausible. They can *see* it. Superintelligence is possible.
