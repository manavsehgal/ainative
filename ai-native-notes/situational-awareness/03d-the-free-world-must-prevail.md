# IIId. The Free World Must Prevail

**Superintelligence will give a decisive economic and military advantage. China isn't at all out of the game yet. In the race to AGI, the free world's very survival will be at stake. Can we maintain our preeminence over the authoritarian powers? And will we manage to avoid self-destruction along the way?**

The chapter opens with a 1924 Winston Churchill quote from "Shall We All Commit Suicide?" presciently describing the possibility of a bomb no bigger than an orange possessing the power to destroy a whole block of buildings.

## Whoever Leads on Superintelligence Will Have a Decisive Military Advantage

Superintelligence is not just any other technology. Unlike hypersonic missiles or stealth -- where the US falling behind is highly undesirable but survivable -- the advent of superintelligence will put us in a situation unseen since the advent of the atomic era: those who have it will wield complete dominance over those who do not.

Superintelligence means having billions of automated scientists and engineers, each much smarter than the smartest humans, furiously inventing new technologies day and night. Applied to military R&D, we could quickly go through **decades of military technological progress** in a matter of years.

### The Gulf War Analogy

The Gulf War illustrates what a 20-30 year lead in military technology can mean:

- Iraq commanded the fourth-largest army in the world
- In terms of numbers (troops, tanks, artillery), the US-led coalition barely matched the Iraqis
- But the coalition **obliterated** the Iraqi army in a merely 100-hour ground war
- Coalition dead: 292, vs. 20k-50k Iraqi dead and hundreds of thousands wounded or captured
- Coalition lost 31 tanks vs. 3,000+ Iraqi tanks destroyed
- The technology difference was not godlike -- guided munitions, early stealth, better sensors -- but it was utterly and completely decisive

A lead of a year or two on superintelligence could mean an **utterly decisive** military advantage of this kind. A complete reshaping of the military balance of power would be on the line.

Superintelligence would compress a century of military technological advances into less than a decade: superhuman hacking, roboarmies, autonomous drone swarms, completely new paradigms of warfare we cannot yet imagine, new WMDs with thousandfold increases in destructive power, and new WMD defenses that rapidly upend deterrence equilibria.

### Decisive Even Against Nuclear Deterrents

The advantage conferred by superintelligence would likely be decisive enough to preemptively take out an adversary's nuclear deterrent:
- Improved sensor networks could locate the quietest nuclear submarines and mobile missile launchers
- Millions of mouse-sized autonomous drones could infiltrate behind enemy lines in stealth to locate, sabotage, and decapitate adversary nuclear forces
- Improved missile defense (robot factories churning out thousands of interceptors for each opposing missile)
- Completely new scientific and technological paradigms (e.g., remotely deactivating all the nukes)

A couple years of lead on superintelligence would mean complete dominance. If there is a rapid intelligence explosion, even a lead of mere **months** could be decisive.

## China Can Be Competitive

Many seem complacent about China and AGI. The chip export controls and the fact that leading AI labs are in the US/UK make people assume China is out of the game. But Chinese LLMs are at best comparable to the second tier of US labs, and even Chinese models are often ripoffs of American open source releases (e.g., Yi-34B architecture being essentially Llama2 with a few lines of code changed).

That said, this is all merely a prelude. If and when the CCP wakes up to AGI, we should expect extraordinary efforts to compete. There is a clear path for China: **outbuild the US and steal the algorithms**.

### 1. Compute

**Chips**: China now appears able to manufacture 7nm chips. The indigenous Huawei Ascend 910B, based on SMIC's 7nm platform, seems to be only ~2-3x worse on performance/$ than equivalent Nvidia chips. While going beyond 7nm (requiring EUV) will be difficult, 7nm is enough -- that is what Nvidia A100s used. It seems like there is at least a reasonable chance China will be able to produce these at large scale in a few years. Most AI chip gains have come from improved chip design rather than process improvements (7nm vs. 3nm), and China likely already steals Nvidia chip designs from the Taiwan supply chain.

**Outbuilding the US**: The binding constraint on the largest training clusters will not be chips but industrial mobilization -- most of all the 100GW of power for the trillion-dollar cluster. If there is one thing China can do better than the US, it is **building stuff**. In the last decade, China has roughly built as much new electricity capacity as the **entire US capacity** (while US capacity has remained basically flat). It seems quite plausible that China will be able to simply outbuild the US on the largest training clusters.

### 2. Algorithms

Algorithmic advances contribute at least half of AI progress. Western labs are expected to be well ahead, with the advantage potentially equivalent to a 10x (or even 100x) bigger cluster in a few years.

But on the current course, the current state of security essentially makes it trivial for China to infiltrate American labs. Unless we lock down the labs very soon, China will simply steal the key algorithmic ingredients for AGI and match US capabilities.

Even if the algorithms and weights "only" put China on par model-wise, they might race through the intelligence explosion more quickly if willing to apply less caution.

**Counting out China is like counting out Google in the AI race when ChatGPT came out in late 2022.** Google had not yet focused their efforts, and it looked as though OpenAI was far ahead -- but once Google woke up, they put up a very serious fight. China has a clear path to doing the same.

## The Authoritarian Peril

A dictator wielding superintelligence would command concentrated power unlike any in history:
- Impose their will on other countries
- Enshrine their rule internally forever
- Millions of AI-controlled robotic law enforcement agents policing the populace
- Hypercharged mass surveillance
- Dictator-loyal AIs individually assessing every citizen for dissent with near-perfect lie detection
- The robotic military and police force wholly controlled by a single political leader -- no more risk of coups or popular rebellions

Whereas past dictatorships were never permanent, superintelligence could eliminate basically all historical threats to a dictator's rule and lock in their power permanently (value lock-in). If the CCP gets this power, they could enforce the Party's conception of "truth" totally and completely.

The author argues for democracy not just because "our values are better" but because he does not know what the right values are, and believes in mechanisms of error correction, experimentation, competition, and adaptation.

At stake will not just be advantage in some proxy war, but whether **freedom and democracy can survive for the next century and beyond**. By far the most common political system in history is authoritarianism. We must be under no delusion that the threat of tyranny is banished forever.

The CCP is a regime founded on continued worship of perhaps the greatest totalitarian mass-murderer in human history (40 to 80 million victims); a regime that recently put a million Uyghurs in concentration camps and crushed a free Hong Kong; that practices mass surveillance and censors all text messages; that has cemented Xi Jinping as dictator-for-life; that touts its aims to militarily crush and "reeducate" Taiwan; and that explicitly seeks a China-centric world order.

## Maintaining a Healthy Lead Will Be Decisive for Safety

Science and technology have always expanded the means of destruction alongside their wonders. The "destruction/$" curve has consistently gone down. We should expect rapid technological progress post-superintelligence to follow this trend:
- Perhaps dramatic advances in biology yield extraordinary new bioweapons
- Perhaps new kinds of nuclear weapons with new undetectable delivery mechanisms
- Perhaps mosquito-sized drones carrying deadly poison targeting specific nations
- A century's worth of technological progress would unfold appalling possibilities

North Korea already has a dedicated national bioweapons program. What happens when their small circle of top scientists can use millions of superintelligences to accelerate bioweapons R&D?

### Why an Arms Control Treaty Will Not Work

An international treaty on AI safety seems fanciful. If both the CCP and USG are AGI-pilled enough to take safety seriously, they also both realize economic and military predominance is at stake, and being months behind could mean being permanently left behind. Any arms control equilibrium is extremely unstable because "breakout" is too easy: the incentive to race ahead with an intelligence explosion and reach the decisive advantage of superintelligence is too great.

### The Case for a Healthy Democratic Lead

The main -- perhaps only -- hope is that an alliance of democracies maintains a healthy lead over adversarial powers. The United States must lead and use that lead to enforce safety norms, following the path taken with nuclear nonproliferation (Atoms for Peace, IAEA, NPT) -- ultimately underwritten by American military power.

A healthy lead (say, 2 years) buys time to:
- Navigate the unprecedented challenges around superintelligence
- "Cash in" parts of the lead to get safety right (e.g., devoting extra work to alignment during the intelligence explosion)
- Stabilize the situation

With only a 2 month lead, there would be no margin at all for safety. In fear of the CCP's intelligence explosion, we would almost certainly race no-holds-barred through our own, barreling toward AI systems vastly smarter than humans in months without any ability to slow down.

When the US decisively wins, **that is** when we offer a deal to China and other adversaries -- they will know they cannot win, and their only option is to come to the table. In exchange for noninterference and sharing peaceful benefits of superintelligence, a regime of nonproliferation, safety norms, and stability post-superintelligence can be born.

## Superintelligence Is a Matter of National Security

AGI is an existential challenge for the national security of the United States. The USG is slowly starting to move (export controls on American chips were a prescient move). But we must get serious across the board:

- The US has a lead -- we just have to keep it, and we are screwing that up right now
- Most of all: rapidly and radically lock down the AI labs, before we leak key AGI breakthroughs in the next 12-24 months
- Build the compute clusters in the US, not in dictatorships that offer easy money
- American AI labs have a duty to work with the intelligence community and the military
- America's lead on AGI will not secure peace and freedom by just building the best AI girlfriend apps -- we must build AI for American defense

### The Convergence of Crises

We are already on course for the most combustive international situation in decades. Putin is on the march in Eastern Europe. The Middle East is on fire. The CCP views taking Taiwan as its destiny. Add in a century's worth of technological breakthroughs compressed into years post-superintelligence, and it will be one of the most unstable international situations ever seen -- with tremendous incentives for first-strikes.

There is an eerie convergence of AGI timelines (~2027?) and Taiwan watchers' Taiwan invasion timelines (China ready to invade Taiwan by 2027?) -- a convergence that will only heighten. Imagine if in 1960, the vast majority of the world's uranium deposits were somehow concentrated in Berlin.

There is a real chance the AGI endgame plays out with the backdrop of world war. Then all bets are off.
